Step-by-Step: Choosing and Prepping a Brisket

Step 1: Picking Out a Brisket

  • Whole packer brisket: Includes both the flat (lean) and point (fatty) muscles.
  • Size: Aim for a 13–16 lb brisket for even cooking and decent yield.
  • Flex test: A more flexible brisket usually means more tenderness.
  • Fat cap: Look for consistent white fat — avoid hard, yellow fat.
  • Marbling: USDA Choice or Prime cuts offer the best flavor and texture.

Step 2: Trimming the Brisket

  • Tools: Use a sharp boning knife and a large cutting board.
  • Trim cold: Fat is easier to cut when the brisket is fresh from the fridge.
  • Fat cap: Trim down to about 1/4 inch — enough to render but not too greasy.
  • Hard fat: Remove dense, waxy fat and the seam fat between the point and flat.
  • Shape: Square off thin edges or flaps for more even cooking.
  • Silver skin: Trim off any shiny, tough tissue on the meat side.
